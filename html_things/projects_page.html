YOLOv8 in Detecting Lesions

The YOLOv8 model was used to detect lesions from raw images of the ISIC 2017 dataset. The dataset can be found here: link to dataset

The model has achieved high precision and high recall levels simultaneously. It is able to correctly detect lesions from the ISIC 2017 dataset with an average confidence of 99%.

Machine Learning Report on the Varying Acceleration of Table Tennis Swings

K-Nearest Neighbours, Linear Regression, and Logistic Regression were applied to data containing the acceleration of table tennis players' swings. The dataset can be found here: link to dataset

Evaluation was made in regards to choosing between K-nearest neighbours and logistic regression to categorise a player’s gender based on the acceleration of his or her swing.

Linear regression was also applied to investigate any potential linear positive relationship between the maximum acceleration of a player’s swing, and the mean and variance of the x, y, and z axes accelerations.
